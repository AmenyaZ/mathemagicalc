Algorithm for approximating the solution of a system of equation using the Gauss Seidel method

This algorithm is valid for non - singular linear equations

Given a system of linear equations such as
$$a_{11}x_{1} + a_{12}x_{2} + a_{13}x_{3} = b_{1}$$ $$a_{21}x_{1} + a_{22}x_{2} + a_{23}x_{3} = b_{2}$$ $$a_{31}x_{1} + a_{32}x_{2} + a_{33}x_{3} = b_{3}$$ With initial approximations $x^{0} = (0,0,0)$

Make $x_{1}$ the subject of equation 1
Make $x_{2}$ the subject of equation 2
Make $x_{3}$ the subject of equation 3
From STEP 1, we obtain, $$x_{1}^{k} = \frac{1}{a_{11}}[b_{1} - (a_{12}x_{2}^{(k-1)})+a_{13}x_{3}^{k-1}]$$ $$x_{2}^{k} = \frac{1}{a_{22}}[b_{2} - (a_{21}x_{1}^{(k-1)})+a_{23}x_{3}^{k-1}]$$ $$x_{3}^{k} = \frac{1}{a_{33}}[b_{3} - (a_{31}x_{1}^{(k-1)})+a_{32}x_{2}^{k-1}]$$
Substitute the value of $x^{0}$ into equation 1 to find the value of $x^{1}\\_{1}$.

Substitute the values of $x^{0}$ and $x^{1}$ into equation 2 to find the value of $x^{2}\\_{1}$.

Substitute the values of $x^{0}$ and $x^{2}$ into equation 3 to find the value of $x^{3}\\_{1}$.

STOP when the Infinity norm of two successive iterates is less than or equal to the maximum permissible error i. e $\left | \right |x-y\left|\right| < tol$
Where x and y are successive iteratives
